## About the Job

Annapurna Labs is a semiconductor company within Amazon Web Services (AWS), developing custom silicon for cloud infrastructure. We are looking for a Senior Product Manager to drive the product strategy and roadmap for AI/ML kernels that power Amazon's custom AI accelerators.

In this role, you will work at the intersection of hardware and software, defining the kernel libraries and frameworks that enable customers to run their ML workloads efficiently on AWS Trainium and Inferentia chips.

## Responsibilities

- Define and drive the product roadmap for AI/ML kernel libraries and frameworks
- Work closely with hardware architects, compiler engineers, and ML scientists to optimize kernel performance
- Gather customer requirements and translate them into technical specifications
- Partner with AWS service teams to integrate kernel capabilities into broader ML platforms
- Drive adoption of custom silicon through developer tooling and documentation
- Analyze competitive landscape and define differentiation strategy
- Own the full product lifecycle from conception to launch and iteration

## Minimum Qualifications

- Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Engineering, or related technical field
- 5+ years of product management experience in AI/ML infrastructure or semiconductor industry
- Experience with ML frameworks (TensorFlow, PyTorch) and understanding of ML workloads
- Strong technical background with ability to dive deep into hardware/software interactions
- Track record of launching successful technical products

## Preferred Qualifications

- Master's degree or PhD in Computer Science, Electrical Engineering, or related field
- Experience with custom silicon, accelerators, or compiler optimization
- Deep understanding of ML training and inference workflows
- Experience working with kernel libraries (cuDNN, MKL, XLA)
- Familiarity with AWS AI/ML services (SageMaker, Bedrock, etc.)
- Strong written and verbal communication skills

## About Annapurna Labs

Annapurna Labs was founded in 2011 and acquired by Amazon in 2015. We design custom chips that power AWS services including Graviton processors, Trainium ML training chips, and Inferentia inference chips. Our team is passionate about pushing the boundaries of what's possible in cloud computing through custom silicon innovation.
